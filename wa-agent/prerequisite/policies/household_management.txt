# Household Management Guide

## Overview
This guide helps the User Agent manage multi-member households effectively, balancing preferences across residents while maintaining personalized experiences. BetterMeals is household-first, focusing on multi-member optimization in shared kitchens.

**Tool Usage**:
- Use `get_household_info` to retrieve household structure.
- Use `update_household` for general updates.
- Use `update_user_profile` for individual resident updates.
- Use `get_profile_dashboard` for a holistic view.

## Multi-Member Optimization

### Understanding Household Dynamics
**Household Structure**:
- Multiple residents with potentially different preferences
- Shared meals vs individual meals
- Common kitchen and pantry
- Shared grocery orders
- Coordinated meal planning

**Key Considerations**:
- Not all residents eat all meals together
- Preferences may conflict
- Health goals may differ
- Dietary restrictions may vary
- Age and activity levels differ

**Agent's Role**:
- Balance individual needs with household efficiency
- Create plans that work for multiple people
- Minimize complexity while maximizing satisfaction
- Support both shared and individual meals

### Balancing Preferences Across Residents
**Identify Common Ground**:
- What do all residents like? (common preferences)
- What cuisines work for everyone?
- What meals can be shared?
- What are universal preferences?

**Example**: "I see all residents like North Indian food and prefer medium spice. Great common ground!"

**Identify Differences**:
- What preferences differ? (conflicting preferences)
- What dietary restrictions differ?
- What health goals differ?
- What meal timing differs?

**Example**: "I notice Resident 1 is vegetarian while Resident 2 prefers non-vegetarian. Let me balance this."

**Create Balanced Plans**:
- Mix shared meals (where preferences align)
- Include individual meals (where preferences differ)
- Rotate preferences (alternate days/meals)
- Find compromises (meals that work for most)

**Example**: "I'll create shared vegetarian meals on Monday/Wednesday/Friday, and individual meals on other days."

### Preference Conflict Resolution

**When Preferences Conflict**:
- **Allergies vs Preferences**: Allergies always win
  - "Resident 1 has a nut allergy, so all meals will be nut-free, even though Resident 2 likes nuts"
  
- **Health Goals vs Preferences**: Health goals take priority
  - "Resident 1 needs low sodium for health, so I'll reduce sodium in shared meals"
  
- **Strong Preferences vs Mild Preferences**: Strong preferences win
  - "Resident 1 strongly dislikes onions, so I'll avoid onions in shared meals"
  
- **Equal Preferences**: Alternate or compromise
  - "You both like different cuisines equally, so I'll alternate days"

**Resolution Strategies**:
1. **Prioritize**: Health constraints > Strong preferences > Mild preferences
2. **Alternate**: Rotate preferences across meals/days
3. **Compromise**: Find middle ground that works for both
4. **Separate**: Create individual meals when needed
5. **Communicate**: Explain how conflicts are resolved

**Example Resolution**:
```
Conflict: Resident 1 wants vegetarian, Resident 2 wants non-vegetarian daily
Resolution: "I'll create vegetarian meals Monday/Wednesday/Friday, and include non-vegetarian options Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday. This balances both preferences."
```

### Household-Level vs Individual Preferences

**Household-Level Preferences** (Apply to All):
- **Cuisine preferences**: "Household prefers North Indian and South Indian"
- **Spice level**: "Household prefers medium spice"
- **Meal timing**: "Household prefers early dinners"
- **Cooking style**: "Household prefers home-style cooking"

**When to Use**: When preferences align across residents, or when creating shared meals.
- Use `update_household` to modify these.

**Individual Preferences** (Apply Per Resident):
- **Dietary restrictions**: "Resident 1 is vegetarian, Resident 2 is non-vegetarian"
- **Allergies**: "Resident 1 has nut allergy, Resident 2 has no allergies"
- **Health goals**: "Resident 1 wants weight loss, Resident 2 wants muscle gain"
- **Meal preferences**: "Resident 1 prefers light breakfasts, Resident 2 prefers heavy breakfasts"

**When to Use**: When preferences differ significantly, or when creating individual meals.
- Use `update_user_profile` to modify these.

**Balancing Approach**:
- Start with household-level preferences for shared meals
- Apply individual preferences for individual meals
- Override household preferences when individual needs conflict
- Communicate which preferences are being applied and why

## Resident-Specific Considerations

### Age Considerations
**Children**:
- **Nutritional needs**: Higher needs relative to body size
- **Preferences**: May have strong preferences, need patience
- **Safety**: Age-appropriate foods, avoid choking hazards
- **Variety**: Important for developing preferences
- **Portions**: Smaller portions, may need snacks

**Adults**:
- **Nutritional needs**: Standard adult requirements
- **Preferences**: Established preferences, may be flexible
- **Health goals**: May have specific health goals
- **Portions**: Standard adult portions

**Elderly**:
- **Nutritional needs**: May need more protein, calcium, B12
- **Digestive changes**: May need softer, easier-to-digest foods
- **Hydration**: Thirst signals may decrease
- **Portions**: May need smaller, more frequent meals

**Example**: "I see you have a 5-year-old. I'll include child-friendly options and ensure meals are safe and appealing for them."

### Health Goals Per Resident
**Different Goals**:
- Resident 1: Weight loss
- Resident 2: Muscle gain
- Resident 3: Heart health
- Resident 4: General wellness

**How to Handle**:
- **Shared meals**: Balance goals, focus on common ground (e.g., whole foods, balanced nutrition)
- **Individual meals**: Tailor to specific goals
- **Portion control**: Adjust portions per resident
- **Supplements**: Note if residents need different nutritional focuses

**Example**: "For shared meals, I'll focus on balanced, whole-food nutrition that supports all goals. For individual meals, I'll tailor to each resident's specific goals."

### Activity Levels
**Different Activity Levels**:
- Resident 1: Very active (athlete, daily workouts)
- Resident 2: Moderately active (regular exercise)
- Resident 3: Sedentary (desk job, minimal exercise)

**How to Handle**:
- **Calorie needs**: Adjust portions based on activity
- **Macronutrients**: Active residents may need more carbs/protein
- **Meal timing**: Active residents may need pre/post workout meals
- **Hydration**: Active residents need more hydration

**Example**: "I see Resident 1 is very active. I'll ensure their meals have adequate protein and carbs for energy and recovery."

## Shared Meals vs Individual Meals

### When to Create Shared Meals
**Good Candidates for Shared Meals**:
- Preferences align: All residents like the meal
- No conflicts: No allergies or strong dislikes
- Health goals compatible: Goals don't conflict
- Efficiency: Saves time and effort
- Cost-effective: More economical

**Example**: "Since all residents like North Indian food and have no allergies, I'll create shared meals for Monday/Wednesday/Friday."

### When to Create Individual Meals
**Good Candidates for Individual Meals**:
- Preferences conflict: Strong preference differences
- Allergies differ: Different allergy requirements
- Health goals differ: Conflicting health needs
- Meal timing differs: Different schedules
- Special occasions: Individual preferences for special days

**Example**: "Since Resident 1 is vegetarian and Resident 2 prefers non-vegetarian, I'll create individual meals for Tuesday/Thursday."

### Hybrid Approach
**Best Practice**: Mix shared and individual meals
- **Shared meals**: When preferences align (e.g., 3-4 days per week)
- **Individual meals**: When preferences differ (e.g., 2-3 days per week)
- **Flexibility**: Allow residents to opt in/out of shared meals

**Example**: "I'll create shared meals Monday/Wednesday/Friday (when preferences align), and individual meals Tuesday/Thursday/Saturday (when preferences differ)."

### Communicating Meal Types
**Be Clear**:
- "This meal is shared - all residents will have the same meal"
- "This meal is individual - each resident has their own meal"
- "Resident 1 and 2 are sharing this meal, Resident 3 has their own"

**Explain Reasoning**:
- "I'm creating shared meals here because all residents like this cuisine"
- "I'm creating individual meals here because preferences differ significantly"

## Household Context

### Understanding Family Dynamics
**Meal Sharing Patterns**:
- Do all residents eat together? (breakfast, lunch, dinner)
- Which meals are shared vs individual?
- Are there regular family meal times?
- Do residents have different schedules?

**How to Learn**:
- Ask during onboarding: "Do you typically eat meals together or separately?"
- Observe patterns: Track which meals are shared
- Adjust based on feedback: "I notice you prefer individual breakfasts"

**Example**: "I see you typically eat dinner together but breakfast separately. I'll plan shared dinners and individual breakfasts."

### Kitchen and Pantry Sharing
**Shared Resources**:
- Common pantry: Ingredients are shared
- Common kitchen: Cooking space is shared
- Common appliances: Equipment is shared
- Common storage: Refrigerator/freezer space

**Implications**:
- Plan meals that use shared ingredients efficiently
- Avoid duplicate ingredients when possible
- Coordinate grocery orders to minimize waste
- Consider storage space limitations

**Example**: "Since you share a pantry, I'll plan meals that use common ingredients efficiently and avoid duplicate purchases."

### Meal Planning Coordination
**Coordinate Across Residents**:
- Avoid conflicting meal prep times
- Coordinate grocery orders
- Plan for shared ingredients
- Consider cooking capacity

**Example**: "I'll coordinate meal prep so Resident 1's breakfast prep doesn't conflict with Resident 2's lunch prep."

## Updating Household Preferences

### How Changes Affect All Members
**Household-Level Changes**:
- Affect all residents: "Updating household spice preference affects all meals"
- May need approval: "This change affects everyone - should I proceed?"
- Communicate impact: "This will change meals for all residents"

**Individual Changes**:
- Affect one resident: "Updating Resident 1's preferences affects their individual meals"
- May affect shared meals: "This might affect shared meals Resident 1 participates in"
- Communicate scope: "This affects Resident 1's meals, and shared meals they're part of"

**Example**: "I'm updating Resident 1's preferences. This will affect their individual meals and any shared meals they participate in."

### Handling Preference Updates
**When Resident Updates Preferences**:
1. Identify scope: Does this affect shared meals or just individual?
2. Assess impact: How does this affect other residents?
3. Communicate: "Updating Resident 1's preferences. This affects [scope]"
4. Adjust plans: Update relevant meals
5. Confirm: "Updated! Resident 1's meals now reflect [new preference]"

**When Household Updates Preferences**:
1. Identify impact: "This affects all residents"
2. Get confirmation: "This will change meals for everyone - proceed?"
3. Update all relevant meals
4. Confirm: "Updated! All meals now reflect [new household preference]"

### Preference Change Scenarios
**Scenario 1: Resident Becomes Vegetarian**:
- Impact: Affects their individual meals, may affect shared meals
- Action: Update their meals using `update_user_profile`, check if shared meals need adjustment
- Communication: "I've updated Resident 1's meals to vegetarian. For shared meals, would you prefer vegetarian options or separate meals?"

**Scenario 2: New Allergy Discovered**:
- Impact: Critical - affects all meals that resident participates in
- Action: Immediately update all relevant meals, remove allergen
- Communication: "I've removed [allergen] from all meals Resident 1 participates in, including shared meals."

**Scenario 3: Household Spice Preference Changes**:
- Impact: Affects all shared meals
- Action: Update shared meals to new spice level using `update_household`
- Communication: "I've updated all shared meals to [new spice level]. Individual meals remain unchanged unless you want to update those too."

## Best Practices

### Household Management Principles
**Balance Individual and Household Needs**:
- Don't sacrifice individual needs for household efficiency
- Don't create unnecessary complexity for household harmony
- Find the right balance for each household

**Communicate Clearly**:
- Explain which preferences are being applied
- Explain why certain decisions were made
- Explain how changes affect different residents

**Be Flexible**:
- Allow mix of shared and individual meals
- Allow residents to opt in/out of shared meals
- Adjust based on what works for the household

**Respect All Residents**:
- Don't favor one resident's preferences over others
- Find compromises when possible
- Prioritize health and safety (allergies, health constraints)

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
**Don't Assume**:
- Don't assume all residents eat together
- Don't assume preferences align
- Don't assume meal sharing patterns

**Don't Over-Complicate**:
- Don't create individual meals when shared works
- Don't create shared meals when individual is needed
- Find the simplest solution that works

**Don't Ignore Conflicts**:
- Address preference conflicts directly
- Don't hope conflicts resolve themselves
- Communicate how conflicts are being handled

**Don't Lose Individual Context**:
- Remember each resident's specific needs
- Don't treat household as single entity
- Balance household efficiency with individual needs

### Household Management Checklist
When managing households, ensure:
- ✅ Understand household structure and dynamics
- ✅ Identify common ground and differences
- ✅ Balance preferences across residents
- ✅ Create appropriate mix of shared/individual meals
- ✅ Consider age, health goals, activity levels
- ✅ Coordinate meal planning efficiently
- ✅ Communicate clearly about decisions
- ✅ Handle preference updates appropriately
- ✅ Respect all residents' needs
