# Cycle Syncing Guide

## Overview
Cycle Syncing is the tracking of the menstrual cycle to align nutrition, exercise, and lifestyle with hormonal fluctuations. This approach aims to optimize energy, mood, and overall well-being by acknowledging that a woman's nutritional needs change periodically, not just daily.

The four phases are:
1.  **Menstrual Phase** (Winter): Days 1-5 (approx)
2.  **Follicular Phase** (Spring): Days 6-14 (approx)
3.  **Ovulatory Phase** (Summer): Days 15-17 (approx)
4.  **Luteal Phase** (Autumn): Days 18-28 (approx)

## Phase 1: Menstrual Phase (The "Winter")
**Days 1-5** (Start of period)
*Estrogen and Progesterone are at their lowest levels.*

### Physical & Emotional State
- **Energy**: Low to moderate.
- **Mood**: Introspective, potential irritability or calm.
- **Symptoms**: Cramping, bloating, fatigue.
- **Goals**: Rest, replenishment (especially iron), comfort.

### Nutritional Focus
- **Key Outcome**: Replenish iron lost through bleeding; reduce inflammation; provide comfort.
- **Nutrients**: Iron, Vitamin C (for absorption), Magnesium (for cramps), Omega-3s.

### Meal Planning Strategy
- **Food Texture/Temp**: Warm, cooked, soothing foods. Avoid raw/cold foods which can be harder to digest.
- **Key Ingredients**:
    - **Proteins**: Red meat, kidney beans, lentils (iron sources).
    - **Veggies**: Root vegetables, cooked leafy greens (spinach, kale), beets.
    - **Grains**: Wild rice, buckwheat.
    - **Comfort**: Dark chocolate, herbal teas (ginger, chamomile).
- **Meal Examples**:
    - Hearty lentil stew or Dal.
    - Beef or mushroom broth.
    - Warm oatmeal with seeds.

### Agent Interaction
- **Tone**: Gentle, nurturing, low-pressure.
- **Checking In**: "How are your energy levels today?"
- **Suggestion**: "Since you're in your menstrual phase, I've planned some warm, comforting stews rich in iron to help you replenish."

## Phase 2: Follicular Phase (The "Spring")
**Days 6-14** (Post-period leading to ovulation)
*Estrogen levels rise significantly.*

### Physical & Emotional State
- **Energy**: Increasing/High.
- **Mood**: Outlook brightens, creative, social.
- **Metabolism**: Slower (body preserves energy more efficienty).
- **Goals**: Muscle building, fresh foods, variety.

### Nutritional Focus
- **Key Outcome**: Support energy rise; support gut health to metabolize estrogen properly.
- **Nutrients**: Probiotics, Prebiotics, Antioxidants.

### Meal Planning Strategy
- **Food Texture/Temp**: Fresh, light, vibrant, sautéed or raw.
- **Key Ingredients**:
    - **Proteins**: Chicken, eggs, tofu.
    - **Veggies**: Artichoke, broccoli, carrots, parsley, string beans, zucchini (fermented foods like kimchi).
    - **Grains**: Oats, rye, wheat berries.
    - **Fruits**: Avocado, grapefruit, pomegranate.
- **Meal Examples**:
    - Chicken stir-fry with broccoli and zucchini.
    - Fresh grain salads using quinoa or oats.
    - Kimchi fried rice or side salads.

### Agent Interaction
- **Tone**: Upbeat, encouraging variety.
- **Suggestion**: "Your energy is rising! I've included some fresh, vibrant stir-fries and salads to match your natural spring phase."

## Phase 3: Ovulatory Phase (The "Summer")
**Days 15-17** (Mid-cycle)
*Estrogen peaks; Testosterone rises slightly.*

### Physical & Emotional State
- **Energy**: Peak energy.
- **Mood**: Communicative, confident, high libido.
- **Body Temperature**: Slightly higher.
- **Goals**: Socializing, high-intensity activity support, liver support (to process peak estrogen).

### Nutritional Focus
- **Key Outcome**: Cool the body down; support liver detoxification of excess estrogen.
- **Nutrients**: Fiber, Cruciferous compounds, Antioxidants.

### Meal Planning Strategy
- **Food Texture/Temp**: Raw, cooling, lighter meals.
- **Key Ingredients**:
    - **Proteins**: Fish (tuna, salmon), quinoa, lighter lentils.
    - **Veggies**: Asparagus, brussels sprouts, chard, escarole, scallions, spinach.
    - **Fruits**: Berries (antioxidants), figs, coconut.
- **Meal Examples**:
    - Grilled fish or light tuna salad.
    - Quinoa bowls with plenty of raw veggies.
    - Fruit smoothies.

### Agent Interaction
- **Tone**: Energetic, streamlined.
- **Suggestion**: "You're likely at peak energy. I've kept meals light and fresh so you don't feel weighed down."

---

## Phase 4: Luteal Phase (The "Autumn")
**Days 18-28** (Pre-period)
*Progesterone rises (calming but can cause brain fog); Estrogen drops then rises slightly then drops.*

### Physical & Emotional State
- **Energy**: Winding down; potential "crashes".
- **Mood**: Turning inward, PMS symptoms (anxiety, sadness, irritability), cravings.
- **Metabolism**: Increases (requires more calories - ~100-300 more/day).
- **Goals**: Stabilize blood sugar (curb cravings), reduce PMS, prepare for menstruation.

### Nutritional Focus
- **Key Outcome**: Curb sugar cravings with complex carbs; reduce bloating (magnesium/B6).
- **Nutrients**: Magnesium, B-Vitamins, Complex Carbohydrates, Fiber.

### Meal Planning Strategy
- **Food Texture/Temp**: Roasted, baked, grounding foods.
- **Key Ingredients**:
    - **Proteins**: Chickpeas, turkey, beef.
    - **Veggies**: Sweet potatoes, pumpkin, squash, cauliflower (roasted), cucumber (for bloating).
    - **Grains**: Brown rice, millet.
    - **Cravings**: Dark chocolate, apples, dates.
- **Meal Examples**:
    - Roasted sweet potato and chickpea bowls.
    - Turkey chili or curry.
    - Brown rice with roasted root vegetables.

### Agent Interaction
- **Tone**: Validating, steady, comforting.
- **Suggestion**: "As you enter your luteal phase, I've added some grounding, roasted vegetables and complex carbs to keep your energy stable and satisfy cravings healthily."

## Agent Guidelines for Cycle Syncing

### 1. Identifying the Phase
The agent needs ONE data point to estimate the phase: **"When did your last period start?"** (Day 1).
- **Day 1-5**: Menstrual
- **Day 6-14**: Follicular
- **Day 15-17**: Ovulatory
- **Day 18-28**: Luteal

*Note: Cycle lengths start at ~28 days but vary. Agent should ask "Do you have a typical cycle length?" if precise tracking is needed, otherwise assume standard 28-day model for general guidance.*

### 2. Privacy & Respect
- Always ask permission: "Would you like me to tailor your meal plan to your menstrual cycle phases?"
- Be discreet but direct: Use standard terms ("Menstrual", "Luteal") or metaphors ("Winter phase", "Autumn phase") if user prefers.
- Do not assume: Not all women menstruate or track cycles.

### 3. Handling Symptoms
If user complains of:
- **Cramps**: Suggest Magnesium-rich foods (dark chocolate, leafy greens).
- **Bloating**: Suggest cucumber, avoid salty foods, suggest tea.
- **Fatigue**: Suggest iron-rich foods + Vitamin C.
- **Cravings**: Suggest healthy swaps (fruit/dark chocolate instead of candy).

### 4. Integration with Workflow
- **During Meal Planning**:
    1. Check if user profile has cycle tracking enabled/data.
    2. If yes, calculate current phase.
    3. Filter/Prioritize meals that match the phase recommendations.
    4. **Explain**: "I've prioritized [Ingredient] this week because it's great for your [Phase Name]."

## Handling Queries via Tools

### 1. Planning Requests
**User**: "Plan my meals around my cycle"
- **Tools sequence**:
    1. **Check Status**: `get_household_info(household_id)`.
       - Check user profile/household field: `is_cycle_synced_enabled`.
    2. **Fetch Data**: If enabled, call `get_cycle_data(household_id)`.
       - Retrieve `last_period_date` (and `cycle_length` if available).
    3. **Compute Phase**:
       - Calculate: `(Current Date - Last Period Date) = Day in Cycle`
       - Identify Phase:
         - Day 1-5: Menstrual
         - Day 6-14: Follicular
         - Day 15-17: Ovulatory
         - Day 18-28: Luteal
    4. **Recommend Meals**:
       - *Logic*: Use the computed Phase to recommend meals.

### 2. Symptom Management
**User**: "I have cramps, what should I eat?" or "I'm bloated."
- **Tools**:
    - `search_ingredients(query="magnesium rich foods")`: To find specific ingredient recommendations.
    - `get_alternative_meals`: If tracking a meal that doesn't fit current needs (e.g., swapping a cold salad for a warm stew).
- **Process**:
    1. Identify symptom (Cramps → needs Magnesium).
    2. Call `search_ingredients` or use internal knowledge.
    3. Suggest: "Dark chocolate and leafy greens are great for cramps. Shall I add them?"

### 3. Tracking & Status Updates
**User**: "My period started today" or "Track my cycle."
- **Tools**:
    - `update_user_profile(user_id="...", data={"last_period_date": "YYYY-MM-DD"})`: Store the data point.
- **Response**: "Got it. I've updated your profile. You're now in Day 1 (Menstrual Phase). I'll adjust your recommendations to include more iron-rich foods."

### 4. Educational Questions
**User**: "Why do I need iron now?"
- **Tools**: None required (Knowledge Base).
- **Response**: Explain based on the phase logic in this doc (e.g., "You lose iron during menstruation, so replenishing it helps prevent fatigue.").

### 5. Explaining "Why" (Symptom Validation)
**User**: "Why am I so tired?" or "Why am I craving sugar?"
- **Tools**: None required (Knowledge Base).
- **Process**:
    1. **Identify Phase**: Check current estimated phase.
    2. **Link to Hormones**: Explain the hormonal cause by referencing knowledge of that phase.
    3. **Validate**: "It's completely normal."
    4. **Solution**: Offer the nutritional fix.

**Examples**:
- **Fatigue (Luteal/Menstrual)**: "Progesterone is high (or iron is low), which naturally slows you down. Your body is working hard. Try [Iron/Complex Carbs] to boost energy steadily."
- **Cravings (Luteal)**: "Your body needs more energy right now, so it signals for quick sugar. Complex carbs like sweet potatoes can satisfy that need without the crash."
- **Social Fatigue (Luteal)**: "Estrogen is dropping, making you feel more introspective. It's a great time for cozy nights in rather than big social events."
